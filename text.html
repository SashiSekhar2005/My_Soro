About Bhubaneswar

A City of Heritage and Modernity

Bhubaneswar's history dates back over 2,000 years, with its origins in the ancient kingdom of Kalinga. The city rose to prominence as a center of religious and cultural activity, earning the title "Temple City of India" for once being home to over 700 temples.

The city's architecture is a testament to the Kalinga style, characterized by towering spires (deuls) and ornate carvings. Today, Bhubaneswar is a fascinating blend of its rich past and a dynamic present, serving as a major center for IT, education, and commerce in Eastern India. It's a city where ancient stone temples stand in harmony with modern high-rises, offering a unique experience to every visitor.

Sacred Temples

Architectural Marvels of Kalinga

Lingaraj Temple

A masterpiece of Kalinga architecture, dedicated to Lord Shiva. Its 55-meter high spire dominates the city's skyline.

Raja Rani Temple

Known for its intricate carvings and erotic sculptures, this temple is often called the "Love Temple" of Odisha.

Mukteswar Temple

Famous for its ornate stone arch (torana) and exquisite artwork, considered a gem of Odishan architecture.

Kedar Gauri Temple

Revered for its twin shrines and associated legends, this temple is a significant pilgrimage site.

Must-Visit Attractions

Explore the Rich History and Nature

Dhauli Giri Shanti Stupa

The site of the Kalinga War, where Emperor Ashoka renounced violence. The peace pagoda offers panoramic views.

Udaygiri & Khandagiri Caves

Ancient rock-cut caves with ornate carvings, which served as residences for Jain monks.

Nandankanan Zoological Park

Famous for its white tigers and diverse wildlife. It includes a botanical garden and a serene lake.

Taste of Odisha

A Culinary Journey

Dalma

A nutritious and flavorful dish made from lentils and vegetables, tempered with spices.

Pakhala Bhata

A cooling and refreshing fermented rice dish, perfect for hot climates, served with side dishes.

Pitha

Delicious rice-based cakes, available in many varieties like sweet, savory, steamed, or fried.

Dahi Bara Aloo Dum

A beloved street food combining yogurt-soaked fritters with a spicy potato curry.

Vibrant Culture

A Tapestry of Traditions

A Blend of Ancient and Modern

Bhubaneswar's culture is a rich blend of ancient traditions and a modern lifestyle. Its religious and spiritual heritage is profound, being the "Temple City" with a history of over 700 temples. This sacred identity is woven into the daily lives of its people.

Religious Tolerance

The city is a beacon of religious harmony where Hinduism, Buddhism, Islam, and Christianity have coexisted for centuries. This tolerance is reflected in its diverse festivals, art forms, and social fabric, creating a welcoming atmosphere for all.
